Real-Life Problems
Problem-1

Arranging books on a shelf:


Answer-1

If you have a collection of books on a shelf that are not in order, you can use a bubble sort-like
approach to rearrange them. You can compare pairs of adjacent books and swap their positions if they are not in the correct order,
repeating this process until all the books are sorted.



Problem-2

Maintaining the Leaderboard in Online Games:


Answer-2

Consider an online leaderboard of video game high scores.
If new scores are frequently added which are typically lower than the top scores, the list is already close to being sorted. Bubble Sort would make efficient work of integrating the new scores into the list.





Some Intersting Facts About Bubble Sort
  • Bubble sort is one of the earliest sorting algorithms taught in computer science, primarily due to its simplicity and ease of understanding.

  • Bubble sort is straightforward to implement with minimal code, making it accessible for beginners.

  • It is an in-place sorting algorithm, meaning it doesn't require additional memory for sorting.

  • Bubble sort can be likened to physical sorting scenarios, such as arranging books on a shelf or
    organizing people by height in a line, where adjacent items are compared and swapped if they are out of order.
